My name is Paul Wright

I am a web designer, developer, traveler, permaculturalist?My favorite definition of permaculture is "applied ecology." The idea is to design a lifestyle and environment that supports both people and nature., and musician

Underlying these many pursuits is a desire to create a more beautiful, better thought out, and more verdant world.

With my wife Kat V. L. Wright I travel the world, working as a freelance designer and web developer. This makes me something of a digital nomad[2]The Urban Dictionary has this to say about digital nomads: "Digital nomads are boss because they are their own boss and can get lost and still be ok.".

We spent the last year traveling around the Eastern USA and much of South America[3]In fact, I designed this website in a cafe in Lima, Peru, and coded a large part of it while camping at a research station in the cloud forest near Oxapampa, Peru. If I needed an internet connection, I had to walk up the mountainside a quarter of a mile and hold the computer up over my head so the USB modem could pick up the slowest 3G signal..

In my free time, I love learning languages (Most recently Portuguese and Ruby), rock climbing, skiing, metal casting and playing guitar with The Missing Parts.

Creativity Logic

Education: BA in Art - University of Arizona

I love making things with my hands, from pen-and pencil drawings to origami nautilus-shell lamps[1]. I try to bring creative, artistic solutions to design projects. I have found that design is as much a language as C++ or Spanish.

I still do much of my web-design layout and wireframing with a pencil and paper, and I even use a wacom tablet and stylus to work in Photoshop and Illustrator because I find the pencil-like stylus to be quick, intuitive and freeing.

I enjoy the challenge of bringing a creative vision to life, whether it is my own, my clients', or a combination of both. I specialize in building clean, attractive mobile-ready sites for small businesses and artists.
